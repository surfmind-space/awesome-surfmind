# STAR and STAR+R answer outlines

Read this when drafting answer angles for behavioral or "tell me about a time" questions, or when the user's stories are thin and need shaping.

## The frames

- **STAR** — Situation, Task, Action, Result. Use for a clean past example.
- **STAR+R** — STAR plus a Reflection: what the user learned or would do differently. Use when the question probes growth, failure, conflict, or judgment ("a time you failed", "disagreed with a manager", "missed a deadline").

## Order the answer result first

Interviewers lose the thread when an answer opens with backstory. Lead with the outcome, then justify it:

1. **Headline** — the result in one sentence, with a number when one exists. "Cut checkout drop off from 18% to 11% in one quarter."
2. **Impact** — why that number mattered to the business, team, or user.
3. **Rationale** — the call the user made and the constraint they made it under.
4. **Actions** — the concrete steps, compressed. This is the Situation, Task, and Action of STAR, told briefly because the result already framed it.
5. **Reflection** (STAR+R only) — one honest line on the lesson.

## Building a headline from one number

If the user supplies a single metric, that is enough to seed a headline. Ask which of these the number is and frame accordingly:

- Adoption or usage — "grew weekly active users from X to Y".
- Revenue or cost — "added $X" or "saved $X".
- Speed — "shipped in N weeks against a M week plan".
- Quality or risk — "dropped incidents from X to Y".

When no number exists, use a qualitative outcome ("unblocked the launch", "got three teams aligned") and label it as such. Never invent a metric.

## Handling story gaps

When the user has not supplied enough material for a question:

- Flag the missing topic by name rather than guessing.
- Ask for **one detail at a time** so the user is not facing a wall of prompts. Start with the result, since it anchors the headline.
- Once you have the result, offer the headline and let the user fill the actions.

## Themes to have a story ready for

Most behavioral loops draw from a small set. Check the user has at least one story per relevant theme and flag gaps:

- A 0→1 build or launch.
- Cross functional conflict or disagreement resolved.
- A failure or missed target, with the lesson.
- Influencing without authority.
- A hard prioritization or tradeoff call.
- Mentoring, hiring, or leveling someone up (for senior and lead roles).

## Worked shaping example

Raw note from the user:

> I led a payments launch but haven't written it up.

Response:

> Your payments launch has no outcome attached yet. What was the result — adoption, revenue, or time to ship? Give me one number and I'll draft the headline; you fill in the three or four actions and we have a STAR answer for any 0→1 question.
